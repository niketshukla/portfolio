At-a-Glance 👀

This product is crafted to assist drivers in efficiently managing their daily driving status, promoting road safety by preventing excessive working hours and reducing the risk of fatigue-related incidents. By providing carrier companies with accurate, real-time data, it minimizes the risk of penalties and ensures full compliance with U.S. regulations.

My Role

I developed designs to help carrier companies and drivers manage their daily data in line with U.S. regulations. This involved a three-stage design process of wireframing, prototyping, and testing, incorporating user feedback and industry standards. I collaborated with Product Managers, UI Developers, and Engineers, transforming concepts into functional solutions.

Problem

Red Dog Logistics have developed a revolutionary framework to track drivers data. However it lacks data synchronization capability leading to non-compliance with government regulations, resulting in fines.

👉 Red Dog Logistics lack a tangible product to justify their framework

Solution

Crafting an application to facilitate driver’s adherence to governmental rules and regulations. This user-friendly platform offers accessible guidance on data management and operation, empowering carrier companies to navigate compliance effortlessly. By streamlining processes and providing intuitive functionalities.

🚀 The app aids carrier companies in managing data and mitigating fines effectively

Research

I conducted user interviews in order to understand users’ pain points on data management. Research reported that it was difficult to get real time data from truck and drivers about their schedules resulting in penalties.

User Interviews

I conducted interviews with 15 truck drivers and 4 representatives from carrier companies. Our findings revealed that drivers face challenges related to accurately logging data and occasionally forgetting to take breaks within the required time period. For carrier companies, compliance checks and audits often highlight issues stemming from insufficient or inaccurate data.

Findings

48%

A significant portion of drivers, approximately 48%, encountered technical challenges while operating mobile applications

90%

90% of carrier companies encountered challenges related to accurately logging data into their systems and receiving reliable information about driver status thus facing operational hurdles impacting efficiency and compliance

User Personas

After conducting user research, we received valuable feedback from our users that helped refine our personas. For instance, Tom expressed frustration about submitting his information while driving, leading to unavoidable errors in data. On the other hand, Lisa, representing the carrier company, highlighted that navigating the complex web of regulations governing trucking operations can be challenging and time-consuming.

I also created a user journey map to better illustrate the difficulty faced by carrier companies and truck drivers trying to navigate through new process.

User Flow

User pain points were carefully considered during the development of the initial user flow for the primary function of the application. At this stage, a single main flow was identified, which was further refined through design iterations.

User Story #1: Change driver status

I would like to change driver status when I start my duty or when I take breaks.

User Story #2: View Current Logs

I would like to see current logs to check total miles and driving hrs.

User Story #3: Report Error

I would like to report an error.

Information Architecture

We designed an initial architectural framework outlining the application's structure, informed by user stories and intricately mapped user flows, ensuring optimal user experience alignment.

Sketches

Following interactions with users and insights gained from user interviews, I commenced the process of translating conceptual ideas into visual sketches.

Wireframes

I translated my concept into an interactive digital low-fidelity prototype and tested it with 8 potential users to gather feedback.

Prototypes

These high-fidelity prototypes were crafted based on the wireframes and all available user research data. Our goal was to create a seamless user experience that aligns with the needs and expectations of our target audience.

Usability Study

As a group, we conducted user testing with a pool of 15 individuals. Subsequently, I facilitated a card sorting activity to discern recurring themes and insights from their feedback.

Product Successes 👏

The final design increased the user satisfaction rate by 35%. Quoting one of the early user testing participant David Miller, "This design feels more intuitive and supportive reducing overheads."

What I Learned 🌱

Throughout my time working on TMS, I've learned the importance of gathering as much user feedback in the early phase of a project, by fostering open communication channels and working closely with stakeholders. As user needs evolved and new insights emerged, being able to pivot and iterate on the design ensured that we stayed responsive to user feedback and delivered a product that met their expectations.
